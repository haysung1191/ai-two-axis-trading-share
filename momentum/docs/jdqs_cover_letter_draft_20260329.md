Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, "Implementable 12-1 Momentum in U.S. Large-Cap Equities: Sector Caps, Liquidity Screens, Trading Frictions, and Capital Constraints," for publication in the *Journal of Derivatives and Quantitative Studies*.

The paper studies a standard momentum signal, but it does so from an implementation-focused portfolio-management perspective rather than a factor-discovery perspective. Specifically, we test whether a transparent long-only `12-1` momentum strategy in U.S. large-cap equities remains economically meaningful after sector concentration limits, liquidity filters, transaction costs, and capital constraints are imposed jointly. The paper compares the stock strategy against ETF-based benchmarks and a same-universe equal-weight benchmark, and it supplements raw-return evidence with walk-forward analysis, break-even transaction-cost diagnostics, capacity diagnostics, and factor regressions with Newey-West robust standard errors.

We believe the manuscript fits JDQS well because its main contribution lies in quantitative portfolio construction under realistic frictions. The paper is intended for readers interested in the bridge between empirical asset-pricing signals and portfolios that can actually be implemented in practice.

The manuscript is original work and is not under consideration elsewhere.
