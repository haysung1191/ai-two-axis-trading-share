# Figure Captions Draft

This file provides paper-ready captions aligned with the current figure plan and exported data files.

## Figure 1. System Overview

**Caption**

End-to-end architecture of the governance-aware AI-silo for cryptocurrency strategy validation. Candidate strategies are generated by a research agent, materialized into executable strategy modules, evaluated through multi-asset, regime-aware, and overfitting-aware validation, filtered by decision policy gates, and only then published to approval artifacts and the strategy registry.

## Figure 2. Candidate Funnel

Data source:
- `paper_results/candidate_funnel.csv`

**Caption**

Candidate funnel for the current research pipeline. The workflow is intentionally selective: many proposals are evaluated, a much smaller subset passes all gates, and only a very small number of strategies are ultimately approved and registered.

## Figure 3. Rejection Reason Distribution

Data source:
- `paper_results/rejection_reasons.csv`

**Caption**

Distribution of candidate rejection causes in the current audit snapshot. The dominant failure classes come from execution-model compliance and baseline performance thresholds, followed by a smaller but meaningful contribution from overfitting-related gates.

## Figure 4. New vs. Mutation Comparison

Data source:
- `paper_results/source_type_stats.csv`

**Caption**

Comparison between newly generated candidates and mutation-derived candidates. The current audit snapshot shows that both source types are active in the pipeline, while their pass rates and aggregate Sharpe values remain close enough that stronger conclusions require a cleaner benchmark subset.

## Figure 5. Cross-Asset Stability

Data source:
- `paper_results/candidate_metrics.csv`

Plot:
- `x = sharpe_mean`
- `y = sharpe_std`
- color by `source_type`

**Caption**

Cross-asset robustness of candidate strategies. Candidates with strong average Sharpe may still fail promotion if their Sharpe dispersion across assets is too high, illustrating why mean performance alone is insufficient for approval.

## Figure 6. Regime Stability

Data source:
- `paper_results/candidate_metrics.csv`

Plot:
- `x = sharpe_mean`
- `y = sharpe_regime_std`
- color by `category`

**Caption**

Regime robustness of candidate strategies. The figure shows how average candidate quality interacts with regime sensitivity, highlighting that strategies with favorable mean performance can still be unstable across market conditions.

## Figure 7. Category Outcomes

Data source:
- `paper_results/category_stats.csv`

**Caption**

Candidate outcome summary by strategy category. This view is useful for diagnosing whether the pipeline systematically favors or penalizes particular classes of generated strategies.

## Figure 8. Run Terminal States

Data source:
- `paper_results/terminal_state_stats.csv`

**Caption**

Distribution of run-level terminal decisions. The dominance of the pause state reflects a conservative circuit-breaker design in which repeated candidate rejection results in human-review pause rather than automatic promotion.

## Figure 9. Mutation Lineage Graph

Data source:
- `paper_results/lineage_edges.csv`

**Caption**

Lineage relationships among registered strategies. Directed edges connect parent strategies to descendant mutation-based candidates, illustrating how the registry enables iterative, ancestry-aware research rather than one-shot proposal generation.

## Safe wording notes

Use language like:
- "audit snapshot"
- "current archive"
- "preliminary empirical view"
- "clean benchmark subset is required for final claims"

Avoid language like:
- "proof of alpha"
- "production-grade profitability"
- "final evidence of superiority"
