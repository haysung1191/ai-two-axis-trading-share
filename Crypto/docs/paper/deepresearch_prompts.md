# DeepResearch Prompts

Use these prompts in a separate research workflow and paste the results back into this repository.

## Prompt 1: Related Work Map

You are helping with a paper on an AI-assisted cryptocurrency strategy validation framework.

Topic:
- LLM-assisted strategy proposal generation
- mutation-based strategy evolution
- multi-asset robustness validation
- market regime validation
- overfitting-aware selection
- governance / approval workflows for quantitative research

Tasks:
1. Find 20-30 relevant papers from 2021-2026.
2. Group them into:
   - LLMs for quantitative finance
   - automated strategy generation
   - overfitting control in trading systems
   - multi-asset generalization and robustness
   - AI governance / trustworthy decision pipelines
3. For each paper provide:
   - citation
   - venue
   - core contribution
   - limitation relative to our system
4. Identify 5 papers we must cite in the introduction.
5. Identify 5 papers most useful for the discussion section.

Output:
- structured literature table
- citation shortlist
- paragraph draft for related work

## Prompt 2: Venue Check

You are helping choose a publication venue for a paper on an AI-assisted crypto strategy validation system.

Tasks:
1. Evaluate these venues:
   - ICAIF
   - CAIS
   - financial AI workshops
   - Journal of Financial Data Science
   - Quantitative Finance
2. For each venue provide:
   - fit score
   - likely paper framing
   - risks
   - submission timing
3. Recommend:
   - best primary target
   - safest fallback
   - strongest journal expansion path

Output:
- venue ranking table
- one recommended submission plan

## Prompt 3: Experimental Design Audit

You are reviewing an AI-assisted strategy validation paper for empirical rigor.

Context:
- candidate generation via LLM
- mutation and diversity enforcement
- multi-asset backtesting
- regime validation
- overfitting checks
- decision policy gates

Tasks:
1. Identify minimum experiments needed for publishable evidence.
2. Propose strong baselines and ablations.
3. Propose statistical tests where appropriate.
4. Identify threats to validity.
5. Suggest the exact tables and figures the paper should include.

Output:
- publishability checklist
- experiment matrix
- threats-to-validity section draft
